# SpikeSort

## Project Overview
SpikeSort is a conceptual tool designed to assist track and field coaches with the organization and optimization of meet lineups. The project focuses on the logic of team-based scoring and the strategic placement of athletes across various events.

## Core Concept
The goal of SpikeSort is to provide a framework for managing athlete performance data and evaluating potential meet outcomes. By comparing internal team data against external competition marks, the project explores how mathematical modeling can assist in the decision-making process for high school track and field meets.

## Objectives
* **Data Organization:** Create a structured way to store athlete personal bests (PBs) and season bests (SBs).
* **Lineup Visualization:** Offer a method for viewing team entries alongside projected opponent performance.
* **Constraint Management:** Account for standard track and field rules, such as event entry limits per athlete.
* **Strategic Support:** Assist coaches in identifying potential scoring opportunities within a meet's event schedule.

## How It Works (Conceptually)
1.  **Data Input:** Basic athlete information and recent performance marks are entered into the system.
2.  **Competition Assessment:** User-provided data regarding opposing teams is factored into the model.
3.  **Lineup Generation:** The program suggests potential athlete-to-event assignments based on specified criteria.
4.  **Review:** The coach reviews the generated suggestions to make final adjustments based on real-world conditions.

## Disclaimer
SpikeSort is a personal project and is currently in the experimental phase. It is intended for informational and organizational purposes only. This software does not guarantee specific meet results, athletic performance outcomes, or accuracy in scoring predictions. All final lineup decisions remain at the sole discretion of the coaching staff.
